FACTION OVERVIEW

U.S. Army forces are professional infantry built around disciplined Fireteams. They win through coordination, mutual support, and controlled tempo. They are the baseline faction. Learning the US Army teaches you the game.

FACTION TRAITS

1

Fireteam Cohesion

When a U.S. Army Infantry Model performs a Move or Shoot Action: if that Model is within 2" of at least one other friendly Infantry Model from the same Fireteam, it may reroll one failed die for that Action.

  • Only one die may be rerolled per Action.
  • This benefit does not stack.
  • The Model must be within 2" at the moment the Action is declared.
  • Vehicles do not benefit from Fireteam Cohesion.
2

Coordinated Fire

When a U.S. Army Model makes a Shoot Action, place a Marked Token on the target. A friendly U.S. Army Model that shoots at a Marked target may spend the token to gain +1 attack die.

  • Marked Token is placed regardless of whether the attack hits.
  • Only one Marked Token per Model at any time.
  • Spending the token is optional.
  • Marked Tokens not spent are removed during the Cleanup Phase.
  • Weapons with the Explosive keyword may not benefit from Mark tokens.
  • A Marked Token placed during Breach resolution may not be spent in the same round of Breach shooting in which it was placed. It may be spent in later rounds of that Breach, or after the Breach ends.

LIST BUILDING

  • 1000 points or fewer.
  • Fire Teams, each led by one Team Leader.
  • Fire Teams must include at least two Models.
  • Fire Teams may only have one specialist of each type.
  • One Squad Leader (Staff Sergeant) required.
  • One Sniper Team and one AT Team may be included per list.
US Army lists reward small, tight Fireteams that maintain cohesion. Spreading models too thin loses the reroll benefit and negates the faction's advantage. Prioritize positioning over model count.
