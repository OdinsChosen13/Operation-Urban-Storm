1 OVERVIEW & BASIC CONCEPTS

1.1 Overview

Operation Urban Storm is a tabletop wargame simulating intense close-quarters combat. Players command Fire Teams maneuvering for objectives across dense urban environments. Victory requires managing psychological pressure as much as physical danger.

Played on a 3'×3' Battlefield. Players control Fire Teams of Models with different abilities, battling over objectives. Each player has one Squad Leader in command.

1.2 Basic Concepts

  • All measurements are in Inches ("). Measure from the closest point on bases.
  • Measure anything at any time.
  • Within Range: distance between closest points of two bases is equal to or less than the stated range.
  • Uses D10. For D5: roll D10, then 1–2=1, 3–4=2, 5–6=3, 7–8=4, 9–10=5.

1.3 Components

  • Rulebook
  • Game board (3'×3')
  • Terrain pieces
  • Models, Loadout Cards, and Activation Tokens
  • 10-sided dice (D10)
  • Appropriate Tokens
  • Battle Deck (3 Objective Cards, 3 Deployment Cards) per player
  • Active Player Card

1.4 Game Setup

PRE-GAME — BUILD YOUR BATTLE DECK

Before arriving at the table, each player builds their Battle Deck of 6 cards:

  • 3 Objective Cards — no duplicates within your deck
  • 3 Deployment Cards — no duplicates within your deck

Both players may include the same card in their respective decks. There is no restriction on card overlap between players.

SETUP STEPS
  1. Set up battlefield. Approx 40% Heavy Cover, 30% Light Cover, plus impassable obstacles.
  2. Determine cover and terrain types with opponent.
  3. Compare point values. Lowest = Attacker. Higher = Defender.
  4. The attacker chooses the table side. The defender takes the opposite side.
  5. BATTLE DECK DRAFT — repeat for Objective and Deployment cards in that order. See Section 1.5.
  6. Set up faction-specific abilities (IEDs, etc.).
  7. Place objective markers and mark zones per active Objective and Deployment.
  8. Deploy Models per active Deployment Card, alternating two at a time, Defender first.
  9. Gather all Activation Tokens and Loadout Cards.

1.5 Battle Deck Draft

The Battle Deck Draft is performed once for each card type — Objective and Deployment — in that order. The same process applies each time:

DRAFT PROCESS — REPEATED FOR EACH CARD TYPE
  1. Step 1 — Both players reveal their 3 cards for this category face up.
  2. Step 2 — The Defender chooses whose deck of 3 becomes the active pool.
  3. Step 3 — The Attacker selects 2 cards from that pool and presents them to the Defender.
  4. Step 4 — The Defender chooses 1 of those 2 cards. That card is now active.
  5. Step 5 — Set all other cards aside. They are not used this game.
The Defender choosing the pool is a strategic decision — you may draw from your own deck or your opponent's. The Attacker controls the final narrowing by choosing which 2 of 3 to present.

2 TERRAIN GUIDE

Create an urban environment: approx 40% Heavy Cover, 30% Light Cover, plus obscuring and impassable obstacles.

2.1 Impassable Obstacles

No Infantry Model or Vehicle may move through or end movement within impassable terrain. Examples: walls, trenches, barbed wire, large wreckage. Players agree before the game which features are impassable.

3 GAMEPLAY

3.1 Turn Structure

Each Turn: (1) Give Orders Phase, (2) Model Activation Phase, (3) Cleanup Phase. The game lasts 5 Turns.

3.1.1 Initiative

Turn 1: The Defender goes first. The Defender issues Orders first during the Give Orders Phase and takes the first activation during the Model Activation Phase.

Turn 2+: At the start of each Turn, both players count the number of friendly Models that have one or more Stress Counters. The player with the lower total goes first for both phases that Turn. If tied, the player who held initiative the previous Turn retains it.

3.1.2 Activation Tokens

Every Model has exactly one dedicated Activation Token. One token per Model, regardless of type. A Model is destroyed when its CEV reaches 0.

3.1.3 Give Orders Phase

The player with initiative issues Orders first. Squad Leader may issue Orders to any friendly Model within 3" and Line of Sight.

ISSUING AN ORDER
  • Remove the matching Activation Token from the stack or bag and place it next to the Ordered model.
  • Issuing an Order never causes immediate activation.
ACTIVATION RESTRICTION

No rule, ability, or effect may cause a Model to activate outside the Model Activation Phase unless it explicitly states otherwise.

After all Orders are issued, shuffle remaining Activation Tokens into a shared Activation Token Stack or place them into a bag to blind pull.

3.1.4 Active Player Tracking

With a shared Activation Token Stack, it can be easy to lose track of whose turn it is to draw. The Active Player card should be flipped to the player whose turn it is to draw from the Activation Token Stack.

3.1.5 Model Activation Phase

The player with initiative takes the first activation. Players then alternate. On your activation: activate a Model with a face-up Activation Token, OR draw a random token from the Activation Token stack or blind bag.

3.1.6 Drawing an Activation Token

The Token indicates a Model type. The owning player chooses one eligible Model of that type and activates it immediately. Place and flip the Activation Token face-down after activation. A Model is eligible if it matches the type, is on the battlefield, is not destroyed, and has not already activated. If all Models of a drawn type are ineligible, discard and redraw.

3.2 Cleanup Phase

  • Remove all Activation Tokens and return Activation tokens to owners' pools.
  • Remove all temporary tokens including Marked Tokens.
  • Stress Counters and Wound Tokens remain.

3.3 Achieving Victory

Victory goes to the player with the highest Victory Points at game end. Victory Points are earned through the active Objective Card. In the event of a tie, the player with the most models alive wins. If still tied, the result is a draw.

3.4 Multiplayer 2 V 2

  • List Building: Each player builds a 500pt list following all standard list building rules. Each player fields their own Squad Leader. Both Squad Leaders on the same team operate independently with full abilities.
  • Teams: Teams are decided before the game. Each team consists of two players. To determine Attacker and Defender, combine both players' total point values per team. The team with the lower combined total is the Attacker. The team with the higher combined total is the Defender. All standard Attacker and Defender rules then apply.
  • Battle Deck Draft: Each player brings their own Battle Deck. Before the draft begins, each team selects one of their two decks to represent the team. The selected deck is used for that team's pool in the draft. The draft then proceeds as normal per Section 1.5.
  • Deployment: Deployment follows standard rules. Each team deploys 2 models per player (4 models per team) per deployment exchange, alternating between teams until all models are placed.
  • Activation Tokens: All Activation Tokens from all four players are used in the same pool. All activation rules apply as written.
  • Faction Abilities: Faction special abilities and doctrine rules only affect models from the same faction. A player's abilities cannot target or benefit allied models from a different faction.
  • Initiative: Initiative is determined per team. At the start of each Turn (Turn 2+), each team counts the total number of stressed models across both players on their side. The team with the lower total goes first for both the Give Orders Phase and the Model Activation Phase. On a tie, the team that held initiative the previous Turn retains it.
  • Victory: Victory is determined by the conditions on the Objective card chosen for the current game, in case of Tiebreakers, Victory is determined by combined model count across both players on a team.

4 GAME MECHANICS

4.1 Line of Sight

True Line of Sight. Crouch behind your Model and look from behind its head. If any part of the defending Model is visible (excluding base), Line of Sight exists. If a Model is 50%+ obscured by terrain, it has cover.

4.2 Determining Cover

A Model 50%+ obscured from the attacker's perspective is granted cover.

Light CoverRemove 1 hit after the attack roll.
Heavy CoverRemove 2 hits after the attack roll.

4.3 Actions

A Model may perform two actions per activation. Each action type may only be taken once per activation, except Move which may be taken twice.

If Stress >= Morale: only one action this activation. If Stress >= 2× Morale: Excessive Stress rule applies instead.

Objective Action (OBJACT)

Perform an Objective action as defined by the active Objective Card. Only one OBJACT per activation.

Shoot

Make a Shoot action using one of the Model's weapons.

Move — Infantry

Move up to the Model's Move value. Partial moves allowed. Infantry may Move twice in the same activation.

Move — Vehicles

Measure vehicle movement from the approximate center of the hull. The tape may bend to represent turning. The vehicle body cannot overlap impassable terrain or buildings at any point along the path. Vehicles may Move twice per activation. Reverse movement is measured from the approximate center of the rear hull at half Move value, rounded down. A vehicle may not move forward and reverse in the same activation.

Window Entry

A model may move through a window as part of a Move action. Traversing a window costs 3" of movement. The model must have at least 3" of remaining movement at the point of traversal. While traversing, the model is considered to be in the open with no cover.

Legal Position

A Model's position is legal if all of the following are true:

  • The Model is fully within the battlefield boundary
  • The Model is not overlapping impassable terrain
  • The Model is not overlapping any other Model's base at the end of movement
  • When on an elevated surface, the Model's base is fully on the platform with no overhang

Models may move through friendly Infantry Models during movement but may not end movement overlapping any Model's base. Models may not move through friendly or enemy Vehicles.

Climbing

To climb a ladder, the Model must begin its activation within 1" of the ladder. The full vertical distance must be covered in a single Move action. The Model must end in a legal position at the top. If the Model's Move value is insufficient to reach the top, the climb cannot be attempted. Elevators do not cost movement.

Engaged Models

Models within 1" of an enemy are Engaged. Engaged Models may only Shoot with CQB keyword weapons.

Sight-In

Remain stationary for a deliberate shot. May re-roll up to 2 dice when Shooting this activation.

Hit-the-Dirt

Immediately drop prone and gain Light Cover until end of Turn. Hit-the-Dirt is the only action the Model may perform this activation. Remove D5 Stress Counters.

Ambush

Declared once per Turn per Model. When an enemy Model declares an action, the ambushing Model may react at the beginning or end of that action: Move, Shoot, Hit-the-Dirt, or a Special Action that explicitly states it may be used during Ambush. A Model may not use Ambush if it has already performed a Shoot action this activation.

'Beginning of the action' occurs after the enemy declares the action and its target, but before any movement or dice are rolled.

Ambush Chain

When an Ambush reaction triggers another Ambush reaction, an Ambush Chain begins. Only one Ambush may be declared and fully resolved at a time. After each Ambush resolves, the opposing player may declare one eligible Ambush reaction. Players alternate until one side declines or has no eligible models. A side that declines is permanently out of the chain. The chain ends and the original action resumes.

Breach

A coordinated or solo forced entry into a building or room through a doorway. Breach may only be declared if at least one enemy Model is visible inside the building or room upon completing the Move.

DECLARING A BREACH

The active Model declares Breach and nominates any participating Models within 1" that meet one of the following conditions:

  • Is the Model initiating the Breach
  • Has a face-up Activation token
  • Has an Ambush token declared

Participating Models consume their Activation or Ambush token upon joining. A Model may Breach alone. Models whose Activation Token has not yet been drawn may not join a Breach as attackers.

MOVEMENT

All breaching Models move simultaneously through a doorway from outside to inside. Window entry is not permitted for a Breach.

WEAPON SELECTION

Before Target Declaration, each breaching Model declares which weapon they are using. Defending Models that have not yet activated or have Ambush declared may also select their weapon. Already activated defenders must use their primary weapon. All weapon choices are locked before any dice are rolled.

TARGET DECLARATION
  1. Breaching player assigns a single target for each breaching Model. Multiple breaching Models may target the same defender.
  2. Defending player then assigns a single target for each defending Model, after seeing the breaching assignments.
BREACH MODIFIERS
ConditionEffect
Breaching — CQB Weapon+1 die, hit value improved by 1 (e.g. 6+→5+)
Breaching — Non-CQB Weapon-1 die, hit value worsened by 1 (e.g. 6+→7+)
Defending — CQB Weapon+1 die, hit value improved by 1
Defending — Non-CQB Weapon-1 die, hit value worsened by 1
Defending — Ambush Declared+additional hit value improved by 1 (stacks)
Defending — Already ActivatedAdditional -1 die, hit value worsened by 1, must use primary weapon (stacks)
ATTACK RESOLUTION

Attacks are grouped by target pairing in the order the breaching player declared targets. Within each group:

  1. Breaching Models shoot — roll dice pool with modifiers. Resolve hits, assign Stress, apply Cover, resolve Defense Rolls normally.
  2. Defending Models shoot — roll dice pool with modifiers. Resolve hits, assign Stress, apply Cover, resolve Defense Rolls normally.
  3. Remove casualties — all Models that reached CEV 0 are removed simultaneously before moving to the next group.

Breach continues until all defending Models are eliminated, all breaching Models are eliminated, or both sides are mutually wiped within the same group. A mutual wipe is a valid outcome.

Embark / Disembark

Embark: Spend one action to enter a friendly Vehicle within 1". Disembark: Spend one action to exit, placing the Model within 1" in a legal position. May not do both in the same activation.

5 STRESS & MORALE

Stress is the primary suppression mechanic in Operation Urban Storm. It represents the psychological and physical burden of sustained combat. Models accumulate Stress from being targeted, taking hits, and from Leadership ability costs.

5.1 Stress Threshold Check

A Model's Stress thresholds are evaluated once, at the start of its activation, before any actions are taken. Stress gained mid-activation does not apply restrictions until the start of that Model's next activation.

5.2 Stress Counters

After a Model is targeted by an Attack:

  • 1 Stress Counter for being targeted, regardless of damage.
  • Additional Stress Counters equal to the number of hits scored after the attack roll and before cover removes hits.
Cover reduces hits that proceed to Defense rolls but does not reduce Stress generated by hits.

5.3 Morale Check & Stress Removal

STRESS COUNTER REMOVAL

When a Model activates, roll one D10 for each Stress Counter. On a 7, 8, 9, or 10: remove one Stress Counter.

Stress LevelEffect
Stress >= Morale1 action only this activation
Stress >= 2× MoraleExcessive Stress — see below
EXCESSIVE STRESS

After Stress removal, if Stress Counters equal or exceed twice the Model's Morale value, the Model must immediately take Hit-the-Dirt and its activation ends. This supersedes the reduced-action rule.

5.4 Stress Exploitation

When an enemy Model declares an action, the opposing player may immediately declare Exploitation before any movement is measured or dice are rolled. Once the action begins to resolve, the window to declare Exploitation has passed. Stress Counters are spent as currency for tiered effects. Once per Model per activation. No LoS required. Spent Stress Counters are removed.

Stress SpentEffect
2Target loses 1 attack die on their next Shoot action this activation
3Target's Move value is halved for their next activation
4Target's cover is reduced by 1 until end of Turn (minimum 0). Light Cover is negated. Heavy Cover becomes Light Cover.
TEAM LEADER INTERCEPTION

When Exploitation is declared against a Fireteam Model within 2" of its TL, the TL may intercept. Stress transfers to TL instead. Exploitation effect negated.

6 ATTACKS

An Attack is any action, ability, weapon, or effect that targets a Model or point and resolves hit and/or damage effects.

6.1 Shoot Action Sequence

StepAction
6.1.1Determine Line of Sight — check visibility from attacker to defender
6.1.2Determine Cover — if 50%+ obscured, note cover level. Cover does NOT reduce dice pool.
6.1.3Attack Roll — roll full dice pool from Loadout Card
6.1.4Hits — count dice equal to or greater than the weapon's Hit value
6.1.5Assign Stress — defender gains 1 Stress for being targeted + Stress equal to total hits. Applied BEFORE cover.
6.1.6Apply Cover — Light Cover removes 1 hit. Heavy Cover removes 2 hits. Minimum 0.
6.1.7Defense Roll — for each remaining hit, roll one die vs DR. Equal to or greater than DR: negated. Lower: wound.
6.1.8Wound Allocation — each wound reduces CEV by 1. CEV 0: Model killed and removed.

7 LEADERSHIP

Leadership does not remove Stress for free. Leadership abilities redistribute, delay, concentrate, or push through Stress at a cost. A Model may only benefit from one Leadership ability per activation.

7.1 Team Leaders

Team Leaders affect only their Fireteam. Command Range: 2" base-to-base.

Passive — Aggression

All friendly Fireteam Models within 2" of the TL gain +1 attack die on Shoot actions. Always active. Lost when TL is removed from play.

Active — Lead From The Front Once per Turn

Select one friendly Fireteam Model within 2" and LoS with Stress >= Morale value. That Model may perform two actions this activation regardless of Stress Counters. Stress Counters remain. The TL gains +1 Stress. Cannot target the TL itself. Lost if TL is forced to Hit-the-Dirt this turn.

Stress Transfer

When an enemy declares Stress Exploitation on a friendly Fireteam Model within 2": the TL may intercept. Stress counters transfer to the TL instead. The Exploitation effect is negated.

  • TL does not remove Stress — they absorb and concentrate it.
  • TL cannot override Hit-the-Dirt.
  • TL does not affect Models outside their Fireteam.

7.2 Squad Leaders

Command Range: 3" base-to-base.

Orders Require Line of Sight

Squad Leader may issue Orders to any friendly Model within 3" and LoS. Orders remain free. Issuing an Order never causes immediate activation.

Active — Rally Costs One Action

Roll a D5. The result is a pool of Stress counters to distribute freely across any friendly Models within 3" and LoS. Cannot target the SL itself. SL gains +1 Stress regardless of roll result.

Authority Override Once per Turn

When a friendly Model within Command Range would be forced to Hit-the-Dirt due to Excessive Stress: that Model may activate normally this Turn. The Squad Leader gains the Stress that would have triggered the forced Hit-the-Dirt. Lost if Squad Leader is killed.

7.3 Vehicle Command & Control

Vehicles do not benefit from Squad Leader or Team Leader abilities unless explicitly stated.

8 LIST BUILDING & FACTION RULES

8.1 List Building

  • Each player creates a list totaling 1000 points or less.
  • Lists composed of Fire Teams. No limit on number of Fire Teams.
  • Independent Units (Sniper Teams, AT Teams) are purchased as part of the list but are not assigned to a Fireteam.

8.2 Fire Team Composition

  • Each Fire Team must include exactly one Team Leader.
  • Fire Teams must include at least two Models.
  • Fire Teams have no maximum size unless restricted by faction, doctrine, or mission rules.
  • Fire Teams may only have one specialist of each type.

8.3 Independent Units

Independent Units operate outside Fireteam structure. They still fall within Squad Leader command range for Orders.

  • Each Independent Unit type is limited to one per list unless stated otherwise in the faction doctrine.
  • Independent Units have their own Activation Tokens and activate normally.
  • Independent Units consisting of two models (Sniper Teams, AT Teams) have separate Activation Tokens per model.
CURRENT INDEPENDENT UNIT TYPES
  • Sniper Team (one per list, all factions)
  • AT Team (one per list, all factions)

9 VEHICLES

Vehicles are Models but not Fire Teams. May not perform OBJACT or score Objectives unless stated by mission. Vehicle crews are abstracted into the vehicle Model.

  • Vehicles have two actions per activation: Move, Shoot, Special Actions.
  • Move: one action, move up to Move value per vehicle movement rules (Section 4.3).
  • Vehicles determine LoS from hull. 360-degree firing arc unless stated otherwise. One Shoot action per activation.

9.1 Vehicle Damage & Stress

Vehicles have Armor Rating, Morale, and CEV. PEN < Armor: hit does not resolve. PEN >= Armor: hit resolves normally. Vehicles gain 1 Stress Counter each time targeted.

9.2 Disabled Vehicles

Vehicles do not perform Hit-the-Dirt. If Stress >= 2× Morale: Vehicle becomes Disabled. Cannot Move, cannot OBJACT, may Shoot with 1 fewer die.

9.3 Vehicle Recovery

At start of activation, Disabled Vehicle rolls 1D10. On 8, 9, or 10: remove all Stress Counters, no longer Disabled.

9.4 Vehicle Destruction

CEV 0: destroyed, remains as impassable wreckage. Explosion Check: 1D10. On 6+: explodes. Roll 1D10 per embarked Model, on 7+: suffers a hit. All embarked Models placed after destruction gain 2 Stress Counters.

9.5 Arc Armor

Some vehicles have directional armor represented by three values: F / S / R (Front / Side / Rear).

CROSSHAIR METHOD

Draw an imaginary line along the length of the vehicle from front to rear, and a second line through its widest point from side to side. These two lines divide the vehicle into four quadrants.

  • Attacker in the front quadrant — use Front Armor
  • Attacker in either side quadrant — use Side Armor
  • Attacker in the rear quadrant — use Rear Armor
If the attacker is positioned exactly on a dividing line, the attacker chooses which arc applies.

10 SPECIALISTS

10.1 Anti-Tank (AT) Specialists

AT weapons built into Specialist's Model Card. One AT shot per activation. AT vs Vehicles: target gains 2 Stress regardless of resolution. Compare PEN to Armor to determine resolution. AT vs Infantry: resolves normally.

10.2 AT Teams

AT Teams are Independent Units consisting of a Gunner and a Loader. See Section 8.3 for Independent Unit rules. See relevant Faction Doctrine for full AT Team unit profiles.

12 OBJECTIVE TOKENS

12.1 Dropped Token Rule

If a model drops a token for any reason — including being destroyed while escorting, voluntarily passing and failing, or any other effect — the token is placed in base contact with that model before the model moves or is removed from the board.

A dropped token may be picked up by either player on a subsequent activation unless the active Objective Card states otherwise.

13 BATTLE CARD SYSTEM

The Battle Card System replaces the legacy objective selection process from previous versions. All game setup decisions are made through the Battle Deck Draft (Section 1.5). Three card types exist. Each card type has its own library of 4 cards in the core set. Players build a Battle Deck of 3 cards per type before each game.

OBJECTIVE CARD

Defines what both players are fighting over. Rules, tokens, and VP conditions.

DEPLOYMENT CARD

Defines where both players set up. Diagram on reverse side.

Full Objective and Deployment card text unchanged from V1.5.0. See V1.5.0 rulebook for complete card rules.

KEYWORD GLOSSARY

All keyword definitions live here. Faction Doctrine documents reference keywords by name only.

Assisted Reload

If the Loader model has not yet activated this turn and is within 2" of the paired Gunner, the Gunner's RLD keyword costs no action. The Loader has one fewer action during its own activation this turn. If the Loader is killed, the Gunner must spend 1 Action to reload as normal.

Combat Effectiveness Value (CEV)

Number of Wounds a Model can take before being killed and removed.

Close Quarters Combat (CQB)

+1 die while Engaged (within 1"). Engaged Models may only Shoot with CQB keyword weapons.

Concealment

This model treats Light Cover as Heavy Cover. Heavy Cover reduces hits by 3 instead of 2. Applies to the model with this keyword only.

Covering Fire (C-Fire)

Target gains 1 additional Stress Counter. All other friendly Models within 2" of the target gain 1 Stress Counter if attacker has LoS to them.

Deploy

A weapon with this keyword must be set up before it can fire. Spending 1 Action deploys the weapon. If the model moves after deploying, the weapon returns to an undeployed state.

Encumbered (ENC)

Reduces Move by 2" (minimum 4").

Explosive (Expl X)

Explodes in X inch radius from impact. All Models within radius resolve hits separately. Explosive attacks ignore cover.

Indirect Fire (IDF)

May attack without LoS. Must make Indirect Fire Test unless target has LoS, or Smoke/Illumination targeting a point. Test: needs 10 to succeed. Miss without target moving: next test succeeds on 9+, improving by 1 each time (minimum 2). Target moves: resets to 10.

Injured

When CEV <= half starting value: Injured. Reduces Move by 3", rolls 1 fewer die Shooting (minimum 1).

ISR (Spotted)

While affected: cover reduced by 1 (minimum 1). Indirect Fire attacks resolve normally without test. Deferred — no current model uses this keyword. Rules pending playtesting.

Lob

Throw grenades up to 4". Roll 1D10: 7+ hits and detonates, 2–3 fails, 1 critical fail explodes at attacker. Grenades: 3 dice, hit on 7+, 2" explosion radius. Ignores Light Cover. Heavy Cover reduces hits by 2 as normal.

Marked Token

US Army faction token. Placed on a target when a US Army Model Shoots at it. A friendly U.S. Army Model that shoots at a Marked target may spend the token to gain +1 attack die. Removed when spent or during Cleanup. Weapons with the Explosive keyword may not benefit from Mark tokens.

One Shot

After firing, weapon unusable for remainder of game.

Reload (RLD)

After firing, the weapon may not be used again until the Model spends 1 Action to reload. A Model may reload and Shoot in the same activation.

Smoke

When deployed, place a Smoke token at the target point. The token projects a 2" radius half-sphere cloud extending 2" in all directions horizontally and 2" vertically from the token base. The cloud completely blocks LoS for any line passing through it. Models on elevated positions above 2" may have LoS over the cloud. Removed during Cleanup Phase of the current Turn.
